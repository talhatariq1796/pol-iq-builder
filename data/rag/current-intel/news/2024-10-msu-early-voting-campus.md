# MSU Campus Early Voting Center Draws Strong Student Turnout

## Key Findings

Michigan State University's first-ever on-campus early voting center exceeded expectations in its opening days, demonstrating strong student engagement in the 2024 election.

### Opening Performance
- **First two days**: 220 voters cast ballots
- **Context**: This was during fall break, making the numbers particularly significant
- **Significance**: First time MSU students could vote directly on campus

According to East Lansing Clerk Marie Wicks: "I'm super pleased to say the two-day total is 220 voters. Students have been on fall break the last couple days, so this is significant."

## Historical Context

### MSU Voter Engagement Track Record
- **2022 Recognition**: MSU awarded "Most Improved Voting Rate" among Big Ten schools
- **Gold Campus Status**: Achieved turnout rate above 40% (7% higher than national college average)
- **Trend**: Consistent improvement across 2018, 2020, 2022, and 2024 cycles

### Why East Lansing Matters

East Lansing is a true college town with unique demographics:
- **City population**: ~48,000 residents
- **MSU enrollment**: ~52,000 students
- Students outnumber permanent residents

As WKAR General Manager Shawn Turner noted: "This is the first time that Michigan State University students will be able to vote on campus. We have more than 52,000 students here on campus."

## 2020 vs 2024 Comparison

A key factor in 2024 analysis:
- **2020**: Pandemic conditions meant most students were not on campus
- **2024**: Full return to in-person learning and campus life
- **Implication**: 2024 provides a clearer picture of the student vote's potential impact
